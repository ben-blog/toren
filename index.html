The Problem

AI agents are already running.
The controls aren't.

Companies are deploying AI agents into real operations — approving transactions, modifying systems, interacting with customers.

But there is no system that answers:

Who approved this decision?

No structured approval. No named approver. When regulators ask, the answer is silence.

Why was it made?

Decisions emerge from a black box. The reasoning is not persisted — and cannot be reproduced.

Can we reconstruct it?

What ran, what policies applied, what the agent saw — none of it is queryable after the fact.

Most companies don't fail because AI is wrong. They fail because no one was in control.

The Tooling Gap

What exists today was never built for control.

Three categories dominate today's AI tooling market. None of them answer the questions a compliance officer, a CIO, or an auditor actually asks.

Observability
Shows what happened

Traces and logs capture execution after the fact. They record what the agent did — not whether it was allowed to, not who approved it.

Orchestration
Manages the flow

Workflow engines connect agents to tools. They optimize how work moves through the system — not whether each step meets your policies.

AIOps
Automates operations

Operational automation keeps systems running. It makes infrastructure efficient — not accountable for the decisions that run on top of it.

Why Now

The risk has changed.

AI is no longer experimental. Agents are now operating at the edge of the enterprise — and that edge has consequences.

01
Executing financial actions

AI agents move money, trigger trades, approve credit decisions. No longer simulations — real capital, real exposure.

02
Interacting with regulated data

PII, PHI, contract terms, trade secrets. Agents read, modify, and transmit — in environments where compliance is non-negotiable.

03
Operating infrastructure

Production access. Runbook execution. Configuration changes. What used to be ops automation is now agent autonomy.

The biggest risk is no longer model accuracy. It's uncontrolled decisions at scale.

Enterprises that scale AI without control are scaling risk.

Where Control Becomes Critical

Where control becomes critical.

Financial Services
A PB agent recommends — or executes — a portfolio change worth $250K or more.
Without Toren

The decision happens inside the agent. The advisor signs off post-hoc, if at all. When KR-FIN inspection asks "who authorized this trade?" — no structured answer.

With Toren

Every action above threshold is held for structured advisor approval. KR-FIN suitability rules run first. The decision record — agent reasoning, policy applied, approver identity, timestamp — is immutable.

Legal / Procurement
An AI agent modifies contract clauses, negotiates terms, and flags risk.
Without Toren

Redlines happen in-document. If a change slips through, tracing "why this clause was altered" requires reading the agent's private prompt history — often already lost.

With Toren

Every clause change is logged with reasoning, applicable policy, and approver. Legal reviews agent edits the way they review human-drafted ones — with full provenance.

IT Operations
An AI agent accesses production systems, modifies configurations, and executes runbooks.
Without Toren

Access is delegated via a service account. What ran, why, and whether it was authorized is scattered across logs — if it's there at all.

With Toren

Every system access is governed. High-blast-radius changes require a human approver. The audit trail is single-pane and complete.
