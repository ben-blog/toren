Who We Are

Built by people
who've seen what
happens when AI
isn't governed.

Toren didn't start with a technology thesis. It started with a problem we watched play out firsthand — AI agents operating inside enterprises with no policy layer, no approval trail, and no way to answer the questions that regulators, auditors, and boards were already asking.

We built Toren because the infrastructure layer was missing. Not the models, not the agents — the control plane that makes running them defensible.

What this problem requires
01
Someone who knows why enterprises can\'t scale AI — and what it takes to fix it

Most AI initiatives stall not because of capability, but because of control. We know why enterprises hit the wall — procurement friction, risk aversion, unclear accountability. Toren is built around those blockers, not around what's impressive in a demo.

02
Someone who has built AI infrastructure in production

Policy engines, agent orchestration, reasoning traces — these aren't academic problems. We've shipped agentic systems at scale and seen where the gaps are. Toren is built by engineers who know what breaks in production, not just what works in a demo.

03
Someone who knows what regulators, auditors, and boards actually ask about AI

The questions aren't theoretical. Who approved this? What policy governed it? What would you show an auditor? We've been in those rooms — on both sides of the table. Toren is the answer to questions that most AI tooling can't even parse.
